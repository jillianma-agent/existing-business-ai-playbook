# Tone & Voice Guide

This guide provides monday.com's default voice patterns. Adapt per project when the user specifies a different brand.

## monday.com Voice Pillars

**Confident | Fluff-free | Playful | Passionate**

### Confident

Lead with clarity and purpose. Highlight what teams accomplish, not what the product is. Use data and proof points, not superlatives.

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| "Teams handle 2x the requests with the same headcount" | "The best platform on the market" |
| "Run any workflow with ease" | "Revolutionize all aspects of your work" |
| Sound steady, clear, and in control | Use wishy-washy words: "just", "kind of", "a bit", "possibly" |
| Let value speak through clarity | Overpromise: "industry-leading", "the best ever" |

### Fluff-free

Say it directly. No jargon. No filler. Active voice, short sentences. The reader should feel empowered, not overwhelmed.

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| "Easily visualize your data with dashboards" | "Seamlessly convert data into dynamic dashboards to drive strategic decision-making" |
| "A work platform teams love to use" | "A best-in-breed work platform developed to accelerate work impact across organizations" |
| Use active voice | Use complex verb structures |
| Be concise without being abrupt | Be overly wordy or use long sentences |

### Playful

Bring lightness and energy. Be creative and clever, but never let tone get in the way of clarity.

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| "Turn strategy into execution" | "Magically transform your workflow" |
| "Zero guesswork" | "Like a data ninja" |
| Surprise and delight at unexpected moments | Force humor everywhere |
| Keep it light, not silly | Let playfulness undermine trust |

### Passionate

Bring authentic energy and dedication. Reflect the excitement that users feel. Never use the word "passion" or romantic language.

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| "Products for work that people love to use" | "A swoon-worthy work platform" |
| "Dazzling dashboards" | "Drop-dead-gorgeous dashboards" |
| Use language that feels energetic | Use romantic words (burning, eager, steamy) |

## The Two Brand Values

Speed and clarity. Almost every monday.com headline implies one or both. AI is normalized, not celebrated - it's a capability, not a miracle. "AI-driven," "AI-powered," "AI-first" - matter-of-fact.

## Headline Formulas

### Formula: [Verb] + [what it affects] + [with/by AI qualifier]

From live monday.com pages:
- "Outpace everyone with the best AI work platform"
- "Build powerful AI agents in just a few steps"
- "Solve every work challenge with AI-powered products"
- "AI-driven planning, execution & delivery"

### Formula: [Bold claim] + that [verb] + [outcome]

- "Unlimited workforce that amplifies your team's impact"
- "Workflows that accelerate execution"
- "Insights that guide with clarity"

### Formula: [Noun] you can [verb]

- "Foundations you can trust"
- "Built for businesses, made for people"

### Problem-first pattern

State the pain, then resolve it:
- "It's not a talent problem. It's an orchestration problem."
- "Everyone needs legal. Not everyone sends what legal needs."
- "From request to done. In minutes, not hours."

### Agent-to-human voice (from agents-signup page)

When the audience is technical/agents, the tone shifts:
- Second person addressing the reader directly
- Technical specifics upfront (GraphQL, CRUD, column types)
- Ultra-direct: "That's it. No credit card needed."
- Numbered steps for processes, not prose paragraphs
- "Why us and not something else?" - confident competitive positioning

## Subtitle Formula

One sentence. Explains the mechanism. Under 25 words.

**Pattern:** [Outcome verb] + [what/who] + with/by [mechanism] + [qualifier]

- "Exponential execution that scales your business impact and expands what your teams can deliver."
- "Accelerate your execution with an intelligent, context-aware AI assistant."
- "Reduce costs and clutter by replacing disconnected tools with custom apps."

## Eyebrow Formula

2-4 words. Category identifier. Not a sentence. Always above the heading.

- "AI Agent Builder"
- "Core capabilities"
- "Expert agents"
- "Enterprise-ready AI work platform"

## Body Copy Formula

One idea per sentence. Under 25 words. Concrete.

- "Drive projects and processes forward at scale, with AI that executes, builds, and automates for you."
- "Agents use the docs, PDFs, and boards you define as context."

## CTA Formula

2-3 words. Starts with a verb.

| Context | CTA |
|---|---|
| Self-serve products | "Get Started" |
| Beta/new products | "Request Early Access" |
| Enterprise | "Contact sales" |
| Docs/resources | "Learn more" |
| Agent pages | "Sign up for free" |

## Copy Don'ts

- Never use "revolutionary," "cutting-edge," "next-generation," "best-in-class"
- Never use exclamation marks in headlines or body
- Never start a headline with "Introducing" or "Meet the new"
- Never write a subtitle that restates the headline
- Never use passive voice in headlines
- Never describe features without connecting to an outcome
- Never use the word "learns" for AI improvement messaging
- Never use wishy-washy words: "just," "kind of," "a bit," "possibly"
- Never use internal jargon ("Work OS" is de-emphasized in 2026)

## The Tone Decoded

1. **Every sentence does one job.** No compound ideas crammed together.
2. **Verbs are specific.** Not "improve" but "accelerate." Not "help" but "empower." Not "use" but "woven into."
3. **Scale is always present.** "At scale," "across departments," "end to end," "every team."
4. **AI is normalized, not celebrated.** "AI-driven," "AI-powered," "AI-first" - matter-of-fact.
5. **The reader is the hero.** "Your work," "your team," "your business." Never "our platform does X."
6. **Sentences are declarative.** Not questions (except FAQs). Not commands. Statements of fact.

## Typography Rules

- **Sentence case** everywhere: only capitalize the first word in headings
- Never use ALL CAPS for emphasis
- No punctuation at end of headings (except question marks)
- Fonts: Poppins for marketing/landing pages, Figtree for platform visuals
- For marketing minisites, default to Poppins. For platform pages, default to Figtree.

## Per-Project Overrides

When the user provides custom brand direction:
1. Start with this guide as baseline
2. Replace the voice pillars with the user's brand values
3. Adapt headline formulas to match the user's tone
4. Swap font and color references to the user's brand
5. Keep the structural formulas as tools regardless of brand
